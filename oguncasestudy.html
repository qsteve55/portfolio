OGUN

Since the birth of social media, there has been a lot of communicating online for people to connect with each other. But there was never a place for advent gym-goers to meet up with each other. Ogun fills in the void of trying to find gym partners that just right for you.

PROBLEM

With different ways of interacting and communicating going on various social media apps. There was never a social media app that targets gym-goers who would like to socialize and link with each other for workouts. Gym-goers was missing a social media suited to them.

SOLUTION

Ogun is made for people who need a partner for a workout session but didn’t have one to schedule, or because, they were new to the area or maybe even shy. But, now the user can meet other gym-goers by searching for other local users, or join/ creating groups that may seem right for them. 

RESEARCH

For research, I created a survey for polling potential customers to see what would they want of this product. I conducted the survey on online message boards and face to face. I received a lot of suggestions from potential users, and with that feedback, I had to prioritize what was mandatory for the app on launch and what can more be added on post-launch.

SURVEY QUESTIONS

  • How can the app pair you up smoothly?
  • What functions would you like the app?
  • How often would you use this app? 
  • Should the app be free or costly?
  • Should the app have gender specifications?
  • What device would you like this app on?
  • Do you use any other fitness apps? 
  • How much of your personal information would you like to be shared?

SURVEY ANSWERS

  • Have an app that allows you group support.
  • Make an app for efficient communication.
  • The app should have a map in it. 
  • A schedule should be included in the app.
  • A workout list should be on the map. 
  • Add a timer to track workouts.
  • Make an option to create a group event.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

They are plenty of fitness apps with group and class support. These apps have a huge supportive base with their customers.I researched their methods for UI/UX design to see why they have a long-lasting appeal. This gave me an in-depth look at the competitors' strong points and weak points.

PERSONA

After the competitive analysis, I created personas. I initially started with three personas but as the final prototype approached I decided to focus on one. By doing this I was able to focus on how I can maximize the user interaction with the app. As well as make sure that the app can still serve the need it was set out for.

DESIGN DRAFTS

I started to create drafts showing the UX design and also potential branding. With various design, this gave me a better look on how would the user interact with this app.

LOW FIDELITY WIREFRAMES/ TESTING

I used these wireframes for initial user testing to see if the app was still heading in a user-friendly direction. What I learned from the wireframes and testing was to change navigation bars, avatar positioning, spacing and a better outlook for a modern design for the app. I conducted my testing on message boards and social media, receiving feedback from other designers and developers.   

LOGO

The logo origin started from a concept of a simple rectangle and a bicep inside of the rectangle. But due to the revision process, the logo changed over time. When I got to the digital design for the logo, I added more shadows and borders to it. But, I deleted that to give it a more modern logo.

FINAL DESIGN/ TESTING

After numerous of testing the wireframe and adjusting the design based on feedback. I started to construct the final design, I kept user-testing the design with each iteration. With this process, a new color scheme, navigation bar, typography style came out. Throughout the final design process, testing and prototyping were very significant to get the app to its’ highest potential.

CONCLUSION

Ogun is an app that gives gym-goers an option to find and workout with other users in their area. This app was created out of my own struggle for wanting to workout with people in my area. But with a busy schedule, it made it difficult for me to meet gym partners. The main problem I had designing the app was constructing a modern UX design. The second problem I had was time constraints, due to that fact some features planned for the app was not implemented.
