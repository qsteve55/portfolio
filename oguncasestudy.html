OGUN

With a lot of communicating on social media, there wasn't a space for gym-goers to connect on. The common group fitness app is designed for people to look for local classes in the area. This alienates a lot of gym-goers that do not go to fitness classes. So Ogun was made for gathering all gym-goers in the local area who wanted to commune and interact with other gym-goers, with no limitations.

PROBLEM

With different ways of interacting and communicating going on different social media apps. There was never a social media app that targets gym-goers who would like to socialize and link with each other for workouts. Gym-goers was missing a social media suited to them.

SOLUTION

Ogun is made for people who always needed a partner for a workout session but didn’t have one to schedule, new to the area, or maybe even shyness. The user can meet other users by searching for other local users, or join/ creating groups that may seem right for them. 

RESEARCH

I needed to see that the product I was creating was wanted or needed in the market. I created a survey for polling potential customers to see what would they want in this product. I got a lot of suggestions form the potential users, but I had to prioritize of what was mandatory for the app for launch and what can more be added on post-launch. The survey was taken from online message boards and in-person contact. 

SURVEY QUESTIONS

  • How can the app pair you up smoothly?
  • What functions would you like the app?
  • How often would you use this app? 
  • Should the app be free or costly?
  • Should the app have gender specifications?
  • What device would you like this app on?
  • Do you use any other fitness apps? 
  • How much of your personal information would you like to be shared?

SURVEY ANSWERS

  • Have an app that allows you group support.
  • Make an app for efficient communication.
  • The app should have a map in it. 
  • A schedule should be included in the app.
  • A workout list should be on the map. 
  • Add a timer to track workouts.
  • Make an option to create a group event.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

They are a lot of fitness apps with group functionality and class support. These apps have a huge supportive base with their customers. I researched their methods for ui/ux design to long-lasting appeal that they have. This gave me an in-depth look at the competition strong points and weak points. Something that the Ogun app can capitalize on. 

PERSONA

After the competitive analysis, I created personas. I initially started with three personas but as the final prototype approached I decided to focus on one. By doing this I had was able to focus on how I can maximize the user interaction with the app. As well as make sure that the app can still serve the need it was set out for.

DESIGN DRAFTS

I started to create drafts showing the UX design and also potential branding. This gave me the initial inner workings of this design. With various design, this gave me a better look on how would the user interact with this app. 

LOW FIDELITY WIREFRAMES/ TESTING

I used these wireframes for initial user testing to see if the app was still heading in a user-friendly direction. What I learned from the wireframes and testing was to change navigation bars, avatar positioning, spacing and a better outlook for a modern design for the app. I conducted my testing on message boards and social media, receiving feedback from other designers and developers.   

LOGO

The logo origin started from a concept of a simple rectangle and a bicep inside of the rectangle. But due to the revision process, the logo changed over time. When I got to the digital design for the logo, I added more shadows and borders to it. But, I deleted that to give it a more modern logo.

FINAL DESIGN/ TESTING

After a number of testing the wireframe and adjusting the design based on feedback. I started the final design. While constructing the final design I kept testing the design with each iteration. With this, a new color scheme, navigation bar, typography style came out. Throughout the final design process, testing and prototyping were very significant to get the app to its’ highest potential.

CONCLUSION

Ogun is an app that gives gym-goers an option to find and workout with other users in their local area. Giving them options to form groups and befriend other users making a user-friendly cycle, workout, partner up, socialize all at once. This application came out of my own struggle from wanting to workout with people in the area, on my schedule but, could never meet people who wanted workout the way I was accustomed to.  The main problem I had while designing the app was constructing a modern UX design. The second problem I had was time constraints, due to that fact some features planned for the app was not implemented into the due to that.
